# Review Criteria

## Core Dimensions

Assess the thesis against these dimensions unless the user provides a narrower or different institutional frame.

### 1. Organization and coherence

Check whether the thesis clearly communicates:

- research purpose
- research problem
- method
- main findings
- implications

Look for:

- repeated sections
- chapter overlap
- missing transitions
- weak paragraph-to-paragraph logic

### 2. Literature review quality

Check whether the literature review:

- is structured rather than piled up
- compares methods rather than only listing them
- identifies a real research gap
- uses citations accurately

Citation mismatch is a high-severity issue.

### 3. Methodological rigor

Check whether the thesis defines:

- problem formulation
- variables and symbols
- reward or objective functions
- assumptions
- experimental setting
- baselines

Weak or incomplete formula definitions reduce credibility quickly.

### 4. Experimental rigor

Check whether the experiment design:

- controls variables properly
- reports parameters clearly
- uses sensible baselines
- explains metrics consistently
- avoids conclusion statements stronger than the evidence

### 5. Innovation level

Judge innovation carefully.

Possible levels:

- engineering integration
- incremental methodological refinement
- moderate methodological contribution
- strong original contribution

Do not inflate innovation if the thesis mainly reorganizes known methods with limited validation.

### 6. Validation sufficiency

Check whether the thesis actually validates its claims through:

- experiments
- comparisons
- ablations or robustness tests
- repeated trials
- statistical reporting

If validation is simulation-only, state that clearly.

### 7. Graduation-readiness judgment

When asked whether the thesis appears to meet graduation expectations:

- tie the answer to the text actually reviewed
- tie the answer to school-specific standards if provided
- otherwise use cautious wording such as:
  - has the potential to meet the standard after major revision
  - not yet suitable for safe submission
  - requires substantial revision before defense

## Evidence Language

Prefer these distinctions:

- observed directly in the manuscript
- inferred from the manuscript
- limited by missing evidence

This keeps the review defensible.
